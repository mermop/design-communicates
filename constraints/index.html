The design process does not take place within a vacuum. We design to solve problems and address needs. We design for people, contexts, and materials. We design with the shortcomings of processes, humans, and organisations.

There are always constraints, and they are communicated in the design whether intentionally or not. If you're clever, you can communicate them quietly and beautifully.

Design is the beauty of turning constraints into advantages.
Aza Raskin
• •

Common constraints

  • Time
  • Money
  • Material limits
  • Technology
  • Ethics
  • Public relations risk
  • User needs
  • Client needs
  • Design capability
  • Execution capability
  • Bureaucratic process
  • Market forces
• •

We need to discover constraints and face them honestly in order to do our best work as designers.
